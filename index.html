Coco Chanel

About Coco Chanel

Gabrielle Bonheur "Coco" Chanel (1883–1971) was a French fashion designer and businesswoman. She is the founder of the Chanel brand and is credited with revolutionizing women's fashion by introducing modern elegance, simplicity, and the timeless “little black dress.”

Important Events

  1. 1910 — Opened her first hat boutique in Paris
  2. 1921 — Launched the famous fragrance Chanel No. 5
  3. 1926 — Introduced the “little black dress”
  4. 1954 — Returned to fashion after WWII
  5. 1971 — Passed away in Paris, leaving a timeless legacy

Key Contributions

  • Chanel No. 5 perfume
  • The little black dress
  • Women's suits with trousers
  • Costume jewelry as fashion statement
